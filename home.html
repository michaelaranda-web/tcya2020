PROJECT PANELISTS

We are privileged to have a distinguished group of panelists for our service learning project. They will be listening to attendee presentations and providing feedback to help make attendee projects the best they can be.

  • Debra Boudreaux

    BIO:

    Debra Boudreaux has been serving Buddhist Tzu Chi Foundation as a volunteer since 1989, with more than 30 years of experience working on disaster relief missions. Debra has led many of the organization’s aid and response efforts around the world: rebuilding of structures following the Haitian earthquake, distribution of rice and food to survivors of Ebola, supporting migrants in Mexico at key shelters near the US-Mexican border through provision of supplies and medicines, and supporting refugees in Thailand and Malaysia through provision of healthcare. Debra has patterned her daily life on the teachings of Tzu Chi’s founder, Dharma Master Cheng Yen: “Gratitude, Respect and Love.”

  • Joanna Zhang

    BIO:

    Joanna Zhang works as Administrative Assistant in Office of Assistant Secretary-General for Supply Chain Management in United Nations Headquarters in New York, where she leads projects on staff engagement and correspondence business process management. She graduated from Teachers College, Columbia University in 2015 with Master of Arts in Developmental Psychology. She is also a certified commissioner in Tzu Chi Foundation.

  • Rosleny Ubinas

    BIO:

    Rosleny Ubiñas is the Youth Representative of the Baha’is of the United States to the United Nations. In her work, she strives to build unity and higher levels of consensus in the various discourse areas of gender equity, race unity and meaningful youth engagement. She is also serving her second term as Chair of the UN Department of Global Communications Youth Steering Committee. Born and raised in the Bronx, NY, Rosleny spends her free time animating the Junior Youth Spiritual Empowerment Program with youth from the area learning together to grow their moral and spiritual qualities through conducting service projects that uplift their communities.

  • Jimmy Yang

    BIO:

    Jimmy Yang has a MS in Electrical Engineering and is a former member of the Avionics team at Virgin Orbit. Since joining Tzu Chi in 2015, he has since co-created the Ethical Eating Day movement, contributed to 2 UN publications on behalf of Tzu Chi (Together We Stand and A Better World Vol. 1), and was on the first of the initial assessment missions to Sierra Leone for post-Ebola recovery. He is currently leading Tzu Chi’s delegation at UNFCCC and UNEA, contributing to UN-related publications, is the primary grants manager for Tzu Chi’s refugee projects in South-East Asia, and will lead Tzu Chi’s climate technology efforts as a new member of CTCN.

  • Steve Chiu

    BIO:

    Steve Chiu serves as Buddhist Tzu Chi Foundation’s Representative to the United Nations. In alignment with Tzu Chi’s work in disaster relief, climate action, education for global citizenship, sustainable development and gender equality, Steve works to build relationships, share best practices and develop programs with other organizations to make tangible impacts on the ground, with the mission of alleviating the suffering of those in need.


    With over 20 years of experience in community based development and local interfaith partnership, Steve seeks to connect the importance of grassroots action to policies that are being developed on the international level.

FAQ's

Is there a registration fee?

There is no registration fee! Simply register here. If there are still openings available, you should receive an email confirming your registration.

If I am not on the West Coast, can I still participate?

Yes, everyone is welcome to participate in this year's online conference. The conference schedule is probably most convenient for those on the West Coast, but the schedule does include breaks during which attendees who want to have lunch and dinner at times that are more convenient for them can do so.

How do I connect to the conference?

We will be using Zoom conference calls to host this year's TCYA Leadership Conference. More details to come on how to connect.

Who is the conference for?

The conference program is tailored to high school Tzu Chi volunteers: from incoming high school freshmen to graduating high school seniors. Of course, many of the group leaders, curriculum team, and other attendees may also be Tzu Ching, Tzu Ching Alumni, Tzu Chi volunteers, etc.

What can I expect from the conference?

The main theme for this year's conference will be Mission of Medicine. Many of our classes and activities will incorporate this theme.

As with past conferences, this year's conference will also focus on promoting team-building and leadership. A major part of the conference will be you working with your group on a hands-on, service-learning project to implement in your local chapters.

Should I wear my uniform?

Although you have the unique opportunity to attend the TCYA Leadership Conference within the comfort of your own home, during the event we still encourage you to wear your Tzu Shao uniform rather than your pajamas.
